# Privacy

Grocery List keeps your data on your device.

Your lists and items are stored in a local database on the phone itself. That's the only copy this app creates. There are no accounts, no sign-in, and no analytics, and the app doesn't track what you do.

## Sharing a list

When you share a list with someone, the two phones keep each other up to date by passing small encrypted messages back and forth. A few things are true about this, by design:

- **No account, ever.** Sharing is a link or a QR code. Nobody signs up for anything.
- **We don't run a server.** The messages travel through free, public infrastructure that we don't own, operate, or pay for.
- **Nobody in the middle can read it.** Each message is locked (end-to-end encrypted) so that only the phones on your shared list can open it. The infrastructure it passes through only ever sees scrambled text and a random label, never your list, and never anything about you.
- **The list lives on the phones, not out there.** That infrastructure is a courier, not a filing cabinet. Your list is always held on each device; the messages are just how the phones stay in step.

## Where your data can go

- **On this device.** Everything lives in app storage on your phone.
- **In your own iCloud or Android backup, if you have it on.** Like any app, this app's data is included in the device backup your platform makes for you. That backup is yours. It runs on your account, which you control. We don't operate it and can't see it.
- **To someone you share a list with.** Only lists you explicitly share, and only with the person you send the link or QR code to.
- **In a file you export yourself.** If you export a list, the app hands a single file to the system share sheet and you choose where it goes. Nothing leaves until you pick a destination.
- **Through Siri, if you add items by voice (iOS only).** When you ask Siri to add something, Apple handles the listening and the speech-to-text, the same as any other Siri request, so what you say goes to Apple under Apple's privacy policy rather than ours. Siri hands us back the finished text, and the item is stored on your phone like a typed one. So the app can offer your lists by name, their names are shared with iOS on your device. We never see or receive any of it. If you don't use the voice feature, none of this applies, and there is no equivalent on Android.

## What's collected

Nothing. We collect nothing, we hold nothing, and there is no telemetry of any kind. The one third party in the picture is Apple, and only if you choose to add items with Siri, as described above. Nothing is ever sold or shared with anyone else.
