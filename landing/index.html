Shop together, off one list.

A grocery list your household actually shares. Add milk on the couch; it's on your partner's phone in the aisle. No account. No ads. No tracking. Free.

Coming soon to the App Store & Google Play

Until then, the code is open — you can build it yourself.

What it does

Type an item and it sorts itself into the right aisle. Share a list with a link or a QR code — the other person taps it once and you're connected for good. Whatever either of you adds or checks off shows up on the other phone. "Finish shop" clears what you bought and keeps the list for next week.

How sharing stays private

Your lists live on your phone. When you share one, the two phones pass small end-to-end-encrypted updates to each other through free public infrastructure I don't run and can't read. There's no server of mine, no account, and nothing collected. It's a courier, not a filing cabinet — your list is always held on the devices.

Why I made it

Every shared-list app I tried made my household trade something for the one feature we wanted: an account, our attention to ads, or a subscription. This one doesn't. It does the thing and leaves you alone.
